Professional Journey

  • 2011: I began my professional journey with then largest, third-party, pure-play Analytics unicorn Mu Sigma, in Bangalore, India.
    • My first client was a global pharmaceutical major based out of NJ, with an impressive roster of commercial products across cardiovascular, diabetes-management, and other therapeutic areas.
    • I was part of the Customer Relationship Management (CRM) team that focused on statistical design, implementation and reporting/measurement of experimental marketing campaigns, with the goal of finding optimal mix between in-person and non-personal promotions.
    • In the 18 months with this client, I had helped the blockbuster diabetes brand improve sales with significant optimization of costs and cross-channel promotional touchpoints, designed and realized ROI from several non-personal marketing experiments, established data and analytics framework to operationalize close-loop marketing (CLM) and taken the client further along on their omni-channel maturity.
  • 2013: I transitioned to Mu Sigma's biggest client, Microsoft.
    • Contributed significantly to the Windows Azure business by optimizing brand and marketing reporting systems, identifying B2C and B2B consumer segments and setting benchmarks for Customer Lifetime Value based on cloud utilization, exploration and adoption data.
    • Developed innovative, data-driven strategies to help Microsoft gain a competitive edge as the organization was charting its business strategy around market needs for SaaS, PaaS and IaaS.
    • I was recognized for my ability to connect data insights with impactful business interventions, leading to my transfer to Seattle.
  • 2014: I moved to Seattle, to work in-person with my clients in the Windows App Store business.
    • Contributed significantly to Windows App Store ecosystem monetization goals and played key role in managing development of self-serve reporting and insights platform, liaising between multiple vendor companies to achieve target milestones.
  • 2015: I joined PwC in New York City to build and mature its Pharma Life Sciences Commercial Analytics practice.
    • Played a pivotal role in delivering diverse projects for clients ranging from niche biopharma companies to large, multi-national pharmaceutical corporations.
    • Managed clients, projects and teams on both large-scale, multi-year engagements and strategic, short-term projects, demonstrating not only cross-spectrum understanding of the pharma life sciences business but also project and people management leadership.
    • Worked on countless bespoke, analytics and strategic projects ranging from few weeks to several months, which collectively covered a significant portion of the Pre-launch and Commercial stages of a drug's lifecycle. These projects have allowed me to develop a comprehensive and well-rounded understanding of the total pharma ecosystem.
    • During this time, I have also actively seeked opportunities at non-pharma sector projects, to learn from and contribute inter-disciplinary and inter-industry perspectives to my own work and that of my colleagues and peers.
    • I have also contributed my time to Pro Bono Consulting work. Youth and education are two causes close to my heart and I have worked very closely with a couple non-profits - CodeNation and Chicago HFS Scholars - to create strategic roadmaps and address their most pressing, immediate problems.

The undertaking required advanced statistical analysis of population claims data, medical insurance survey insights for development of patient utilization curves.

    • Early and continued exposure to diverse business-driven, strategic technology problems have made me a well-rounded, adaptive, analytics and decision sciences professional with a solid understanding of various functional areas in Pharma (and other industries), their needs and priorities.
